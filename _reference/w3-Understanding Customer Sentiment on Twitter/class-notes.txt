import requests

headers = {"Authorization": f"Bearer YOUR_HF_TOKEN"}

def query(payload):

def top_3_words(text):
    words = text.lower().split()
    freq = Counter(words)
    return freq.most_common(3)

Introducing Image Generation
Image generation refers to the process of creating visual content using AI tools. In some cases, it means turning text prompts into images—like asking a model to draw "a cat on a skateboard in space." In other cases, it means automatically creating data visualizations. For instance, like turning a messy table of numbers into clean, insightful charts and graphs.
Both of these are examples of image generation, and both are useful in different ways.
Why Does Image Generation Matter in Business?
Obviously, automation is always great but are the concrete benefits that businesses can harness if the best practices from image generation are introduced? From a business perspective, image generation can:
Speed up decision-making: Charts and visual summaries make complex data easier to understand, especially for stakeholders who aren’t data experts.
Save time and resources: Instead of manually creating visuals, AI can automate the process, letting teams focus on interpreting results.
Improve communication: A picture is worth a thousand words—especially when explaining patterns, trends, or forecasts.
Support marketing, design, and innovation: Generated images can be used in content creation, product concepts, or brand visuals. Therefore, saving costs on photography or design.
In short, image generation helps businesses move faster, explain ideas better, and unlock creativity with fewer constraints.
