Information on Project Evaluation
 
Below you’ll find a few important things to know before submitting and presenting your project. Taking a moment to read this now will help things go smoothly.
Evaluation & Grading
Grades are based on the quality of the presentation, including:
Understanding of the problem
Explanation of methodology and decisions
Interpretation of results and insights
Ability to answer questions
Grades are individual, even for group projects.
This means grades may vary between students depending on individual contribution and presentation performance.
If you can’t attend the presentation or you don’t pass it, your case will be reviewed ad hoc with your ASM.
Project Submission
You are required to submit all artifacts listed in the project description.
Even though the project is evaluated based on the presentation, submitting all required artifacts is mandatory.
The final submission must be completed before the presentation session starts.
Project Presentation
Project evaluation is primarily based on the live presentation.
Every student must present their project (or their part of a group project).
The presentation takes place during the dedicated presentation session of the course.
During the session, the instructor:
Reviews the presentation live
Asks questions
Provides live feedback
Code Clinic Sessions
Each course includes one Code Clinic session.
Code Clinics give you feedback on your project’s technical quality and let you learn from other students’ work.
During these sessions, you can receive feedback on topics such as:
Code structure and readability
Best practices
Performance and efficiency
Technical correctness
⚠️
Code Clinics are support sessions and are not used for final grading. The final evaluation happens during the presentation.
 
Key Takeaways
Submit all required artifacts in Codio
Submit before the presentation session
Every student must present
Final evaluation is based on the presentation
Use Code Clinics to strengthen the technical quality of your portfolio projects and learn from your improvements and from other student’s projects too.
